Aerodynamic Performance Analysis of Helicopter Rotor Systems

A comparative computational study of the Bell 206B JetRanger and AgustaWestland AW139 using Blade Element Theory (BET) and Actuator Disc Theory, implemented in MATLAB. The report covers four phases: baseline lift modelling, non-linear stall behaviour, two-aircraft comparison, and engine power/Figure of Merit analysis. Confirmed results: Bell 206B total hover power 72.8 kW (FM = 0.680), AW139 total hover power 287.7 kW (FM = 0.780). Linear model overestimates lift by 67% above stall angle. AW139 requires only 0.045 kW/kg vs Bell 206B's 0.050 kW/kg — demonstrating superior disc efficiency.

Performance Plots

All 14 MATLAB figures generated across the four project phases — from baseline lift curves to Figure of Merit analysis.

Phase 1 — Baseline Lift Model (Plots 1–4)
Plot 1 — Rotor Lift vs RPM
Lift scales quadratically with RPM. At 450 RPM (pitch = 8°): ~27 kN for a 2-bladed rotor with R = 5.0 m, c = 0.30 m.
Max: ~27 kN @ 450 RPM
Plot 2 — Lift vs Pitch Angle
Linear relationship confirmed in pre-stall regime (0–14°). At RPM = 300: lift rises from 0 to ~23 kN at 14° pitch.
Linear: 0 → ~23 kN (0–14°)
Plot 3 — 3D Performance Surface
Full operating envelope mapped across RPM (200–450) and pitch (0–12°). Jet colormap — peak lift at top-right corner.
Surface: RPM × Pitch → Lift (kN)
Plot 4 — Spanwise Lift Distribution
Tip (final 30% span) generates ~65% of total lift. Tip lift: ~4,000 N/m vs hub region ~50 N/m — 80× differential.
Tip peak: ~4,000 N/m
Phase 2A — Stall Modelling (Plots 5–7)
Plot 5 — Non-linear Cl vs Pitch
Piecewise lift coefficient model: linear to 14°, smooth drop 14–17°, post-stall plateau at 60% of Cl_max above 17°.
Stall onset: 14° | Post-stall: 60% Cl_max
Plot 6 — Linear vs Stall Model
Linear model (blue dashed) diverges sharply above 14°. At 20° pitch: linear overestimates lift by 67% vs physically accurate stall model (red).
Error at 20°: +67% overestimate
Plot 7 — 3D Surface with Stall Boundary
White line delineates safe operating envelope from stall-risk zone. Analogous to HIGE/HOGE envelopes in helicopter Flight Manuals.
Stall boundary: white line @ 14°
Phase 2B — Bell 206B vs AW139 (Plots 8–10)
Plot 8 — Lift vs RPM: 206B vs AW139
AW139 (red, lower RPM range) generates far more lift than Bell 206B (blue). Both exceed MTOW hover threshold (dashed lines) confirming positive lift margins.
AW139: ~4.4× more lift than 206B
Plot 9 — Lift vs Pitch: Both Aircraft
Comparison at cruise RPM (Bell 206B: 355 RPM, AW139: 245 RPM). Stall onset at 14° clearly marked. Both show positive lift margin above MTOW dashed lines.
Stall: 14° | Both above MTOW threshold
Plot 10 — Spanwise Distribution (Normalised)
0–100% normalised span. AW139 (blue) peaks at ~7,000 N/m vs Bell 206B (red) at ~6,000 N/m at tip. Same tip-dominant pattern for both aircraft.
AW139 tip: ~7,000 N/m | 206B: ~6,000 N/m
Phase 2C — Power & Figure of Merit (Plots 11–14)
Plot 11 — Power Required vs RPM
Total hover power at MTOW across operational RPM range. Bell 206B: 72.8 kW max. AW139: 287.7 kW max. AW139 requires ~3.95× more absolute power.
206B: 72.8 kW | AW139: 287.7 kW
Plot 12 — Power Breakdown (Bar chart)
Induced power (blue) vs Profile power (orange) for each aircraft. Bell 206B: Pi = 58.2 kW, P0 = 14.6 kW. AW139: Pi = 198.4 kW, P0 = 89.3 kW.
Pi dominates in both aircraft
Plot 13 — Power vs Weight Fraction
AW139 reaches ~1,000 kW at MTOW (100%), dropping to ~590 kW at 70% MTOW. Bell 206B: ~200 kW at MTOW, ~118 kW at 70%. Power reduces non-linearly with weight.
At 70% MTOW: ~59% of hover power
Plot 14 — Figure of Merit ★
Key efficiency metric. Bell 206B FM = 0.680 (below 0.75 threshold). AW139 FM = 0.780 (above threshold — excellent). FM differential of 0.100 attributed to disc loading and blade geometry.
FM: 0.680 (206B) vs 0.780 (AW139) ★
